## Description
Glia is a native macOS window into your knowledge graph — built for
gbrain, happy with any graph export.

Every page becomes a lit orb, every connection a thread. The layout is
stable: your brain's geography stays where you learned it, and new
knowledge blooms in beside what it relates to.

• A living map — GPU-rendered constellation of your pages, colored by
  type, sized by connectivity, with your unlinked notes as a faint halo
  of dark matter around the structure
• Growth replay — scrub through time and watch months of accumulated
  knowledge assemble itself in seconds
• Instant search — ⌘K, type, return: the camera flies to the page
• Focus mode — click any node to light up its neighborhood and read the
  full page, facts and connections included
• Deeply native — Spotlight indexes every page, Shortcuts support,
  menu-bar status, Reduce Motion / Reduce Transparency respected
• Private by architecture — your knowledge never leaves your Mac

New here? Tap "Explore the Demo" to wander a sample brain before
connecting your own.

Glia is open source (MIT).

required: press "Explore the Demo" on first launch to load the bundled
sample dataset and exercise every feature (search, focus, growth replay).

Brain Folder (security-scoped bookmark; read-only).
